What is Ethnobotany?

The study of bioregional plants and their practical uses through the traditional knowledge of local, indigenous culture and people.


What does it have to do with BIPOC?

“Ethnobotanists are largely concerned with intellectual property rights and benefits sharing agreements. In the US, plant knowledge + agricultural knowledge in the form of oral traditions created by black and brown folks are largely uncredited. The lives and life work of black folks matter.” -@indyofficinalis


Why should we care?

We cannot dismantle systemic racism without acknowledging the unpaid labor and uncredited work of POC in agricultural, foraging, and mycology practices. Especially right now, we have to honor POC that laid the foundation for our current understanding of plant and fungi edibility and usefulness.


What do we do?

As the popularity of urban agriculture, foraging, and homesteading increases especially during this pandemic where people have recently found the urge to be more self-sustaining, we want to use this platform to amplify the voices of our black and brown ancestors, who were often uncredited, overlooked, disenfranchised or exploited as well as promoting and encouraging modern-day farmers or plant lovers just like you and me.
